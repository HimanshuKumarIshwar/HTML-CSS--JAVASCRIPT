1.1 BACKGROUND

  • A refrigerator is a common household appliance that is used to store perishable food at a low temperature, preserving its freshness and preventing spoilage. The purpose of a refrigerator is to keep the temperature inside the appliance low enough to inhibit the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms that can cause food to spoil.
  • A static model of a refrigerator is a simplified representation of the system that is used to analyze and understand its behavior under different operating conditions. Static models do not take into account the time-varying nature of the system, but rather focus on the relationships between the variables at a specific point in time.
  • Static models are commonly used in engineering and design to analyze and predict the behavior of a system without the need for complex and time-consuming simulations. They are useful for understanding the basic principles of a system and for identifying the key factors that influence its performance. Static models can also be used to optimize the design of a system by identifying the parameters that have the greatest impact on its performance.
